UNGLI (‘The finger’)
Ungli (“The Finger”) was born out of personally witnessing multiple incidents of children and less previleged youths being mistreated and manhandled in public. Bystander effect is quite common in these cases - as such incidents have sunk into the psyche of the society as an everyday occurance. These are the crimes that we define as 'those which people see, momentarily rile against in their internal monologue, but do nothing about as they slip back into their everyday lives'. The research conducted by a team of 3 (including myself) to explore the possiblity of citizen sourced crime reporting. The desired outcome is for a society to become aware of its internal dysfunctions and act against them, however discreetly. Ungli - the business plan figured under Eureka 2015, Indian Institute of Technology Bombay.

Some Insights (Post scenario-primary research)
Problem Statement: Playscapes are diminishing due to the effects of urbanisation limiting space, diminishing due to the infusion of substitutes in the form of technology (quote: touch-screens. internet, mobile phones).  Problem statement naturally follows: “How to infuse technology in a synergetic manner to help assist (networked) playscapes in order to bring more people together to have a playful experience?”. Synchronous walk is an outdoor installation that innocuously tickles people’s curiosity and brings them together. When two people walk with their footfalls in sync on the plates (as seen in the picture), nothing happens. If not, there’s a startling announcement on the speaker.  At MIT Media Labs workshop 2015.
